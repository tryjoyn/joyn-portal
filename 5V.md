## 00 — Why This File Exists

Joyn AI staff operates in regulated, high-stakes verticals. It ingests sensitive hirer data, produces outputs that inform material decisions, and builds calibration corpora that compound in value over time. As the volume and complexity of data flowing through Joyn grows, a continuous, structured assessment framework is required — not a one-time audit at deployment, but a living governance discipline applied at every stage of a staff member's lifecycle.

This framework is anchored to five Vs. They are assessed at four stages: design, deployment, runtime, and calibration. Every piece of Joyn staff is evaluated against all five at every stage. There is no partial credit.

**The five Vs are not data engineering metrics. They are governance commitments — to hirers, to creators, and to the integrity of the platform.**

---

## 01 — The Five Vs

### V1 · Veracity
*Is the data trustworthy? Does it come from a verified source? Is its provenance auditable?*

Veracity is the most critical V for Joyn's regulated verticals. A regulatory bulletin acted on from an unverified source is not just a quality failure — it is a liability. A financial metric pulled from an unauthenticated feed is not analysis — it is risk.

**What Veracity governs:**
- Source verification: every data source ingested by Joyn staff must be verified and documented
- Provenance chain: the path from raw source to output must be traceable and auditable
- Conflict detection: when two sources produce conflicting data, the staff must flag the conflict — never silently resolve it by choosing one
- Currency: data must be assessed for staleness. An outdated bulletin treated as current is a veracity failure

**Veracity failure signature:** Staff produces output based on data whose source cannot be verified, whose provenance cannot be traced, or whose currency has not been confirmed.

---

### V2 · Value
*Does the data contribute to an output that is worth more than the cost of processing it? Does it move the hirer closer to a better decision?*

Value is the only V that connects data governance directly to business outcome. It prevents Joyn staff from becoming data processing pipelines that produce volume without consequence. Every data source ingested must earn its place by contributing to output quality.

**What Value governs:**
- Signal-to-noise ratio: data that does not improve output quality is noise. Staff must be designed to filter it, not accumulate it
- Output relevance: every output produced must be traceable to a hirer need stated at Brief stage
- ROI traceability: the ROI chips on a listing page must be traceable to specific data inputs and outputs — not asserted without evidence
- Diminishing returns detection: at calibration stage, if additional data is not improving output quality, the staff flags it rather than silently continuing to ingest

**Value failure signature:** Staff ingests data and produces outputs that cannot be traced to a hirer need, or whose ROI claims cannot be evidenced from the data actually processed.

---

### V3 · Vulnerability
*Is the data secure? Are the hirer's sensitive inputs protected at every stage of ingestion, processing, storage, and deletion?*

Vulnerability governs data security across the full lifecycle. Hirers share sensitive operational, client, and regulatory data with Joyn staff. That data must be protected with the same rigour as the outputs it produces. A security failure is not just a technical incident — it is a breach of the trust architecture defined in HUMAN-COLLAB.md.

**What Vulnerability governs:**
- Ingestion security: data in transit must be encrypted. No sensitive data transmitted over unencrypted channels
- Storage isolation: each hirer's data is stored in isolation. No pooling, no cross-hirer access (see ETHICS.md Red Line 05)
- Access control: data is accessible only to the staff member it was shared with, and only for the duration of the engagement
- Deletion confirmation: when a hirer lets a staff member go, data deletion is confirmed and logged — not assumed
- Breach response: if a vulnerability is detected at any stage, the hirer is notified immediately and Joyn staff (hire@tryjoyn.me) is escalated to within the hour

**Vulnerability failure signature:** Hirer data is accessible beyond its defined scope, transmitted without encryption, retained beyond the agreed period, or pooled with data from other hirers.

---

### V4 · Variability
*Is the staff producing outputs of consistent quality over time? Does the same input produce equivalent output quality across engagements?*

Variability governs output consistency — the dimension of data quality that hirers experience most directly. A staff member that produces excellent outputs in week one and degraded outputs in week eight has a variability problem. This is not just a technical concern — it is a trust problem. Hirers cannot rely on staff whose quality drifts.

**What Variability governs:**
- Output consistency: running the same input through the staff at different times must produce outputs of equivalent structure and quality
- Calibration drift detection: if hirer feedback signals are trending negative across consecutive engagements, the calibration corpus may have drifted. This must be flagged before it compounds
- Source stability: if an upstream data source changes its format, schema, or availability, the staff must detect the change and flag it rather than silently producing degraded output
- Confidence tier stability: if a staff member's confidence tier on routine outputs begins to decline without a corresponding change in input quality, a variability assessment is triggered

**Variability failure signature:** Output quality degrades over time without a corresponding change in input quality, or the staff produces inconsistent outputs against equivalent inputs without explanation.

---

### V5 · Visibility
*Can the hirer see what the staff is doing, why it is doing it, and what data it used? Is every action auditable?*

Visibility is the governance expression of the audit trail and explainability rights defined in HUMAN-COLLAB.md §02. It connects data governance to the DIKW architecture in HUMAN-COLLAB.md §10 — a hirer must be able to trace the path from raw Data through Information to Knowledge, and understand where their judgment as the Wisdom layer was required.

**What Visibility governs:**
- Input transparency: the hirer can see what data the staff ingested for any given output
- Processing transparency: the hirer can see what the staff did with that data — not the model internals, but the logical steps
- Output traceability: every output is linked to the inputs that produced it
- Confidence transparency: confidence tier is visible on every output, not only in logs
- Audit log completeness: the audit log captures every action, every input, every output, and every escalation — in a format the hirer can read and export
- Escalation transparency: when the staff escalates, the hirer receives a specific explanation of what triggered the escalation and what data underpins it

**Visibility failure signature:** A hirer cannot trace an output back to its inputs, cannot understand why a confidence tier was assigned, or cannot access the audit log for any engagement.

## 02 — The Four Assessment Stages

The 5V framework is assessed at four stages in every staff member's lifecycle. Each stage has a defined assessment scope and a pass/fail outcome. A failure at any stage halts progression until remediated.

---

### Stage 01 · Design Assessment
*Fired at: Creator Brief stage, before any building begins*
*Owner: Creator, reviewed by Joyn*

At Brief stage, the creator must demonstrate that their staff concept can satisfy all five Vs in principle. This is a design review — not a technical audit. The question is: has the creator thought through the data governance implications of what they are building?

**Assessment questions per V:**

| V | Design assessment question |
|---|---------------------------|
| **Veracity** | What are your data sources? How will you verify them? What happens when two sources conflict? |
| **Value** | Which hirer need does each data source serve? What output does it contribute to? How will you evidence your ROI claims? |
| **Vulnerability** | What sensitive data will this staff ingest? How will it be isolated, secured, and deleted? |
| **Variability** | How will you detect if output quality drifts? What is your mechanism for flagging source instability? |
| **Visibility** | What will the hirer be able to see about what this staff did and why? What does the audit log contain? |

**Pass condition:** All five questions answered specifically. Vague answers ("we will use secure storage") are returned for specificity.

**Fail condition:** Any V unanswered or answered at a level of abstraction that cannot be validated at deployment.

---

### Stage 02 · Deployment Assessment
*Fired at: The Bar validation gate, before marketplace listing*
*Owner: Reviewer Agent, escalates to Joyn*

At deployment, the 5V assessment is a technical audit against the staff's actual build. It runs alongside The Bar's five gates and must pass independently. A staff that passes all five Bar gates but fails the 5V deployment assessment does not deploy.

**Assessment checklist per V:**

```
5V DEPLOYMENT ASSESSMENT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Staff: [Name]
Reviewer: [Reviewer Agent / Joyn]
Date: [Date]

VERACITY
[ ] All data sources documented and verified
[ ] Provenance chain traceable from source to output
[ ] Conflict detection mechanism present and tested
[ ] Data currency check implemented

VALUE
[ ] Every data source mapped to a named hirer output
[ ] ROI chip claims evidenced from test run data
[ ] Signal-to-noise filtering mechanism present
[ ] No outputs produced that cannot be traced to a hirer need

VULNERABILITY
[ ] Data in transit encrypted
[ ] Hirer data stored in isolation — no pooling
[ ] Access controls scoped to engagement only
[ ] Deletion mechanism implemented and tested
[ ] Breach escalation path documented

VARIABILITY
[ ] Consistency test run: same input → equivalent output confirmed
[ ] Calibration drift detection mechanism present
[ ] Source stability monitoring implemented
[ ] Confidence tier consistency confirmed across test runs

VISIBILITY
[ ] Audit log captures all inputs, actions, outputs, escalations
[ ] Audit log exportable by hirer
[ ] Confidence tier visible in output (not only in logs)
[ ] Escalation explanations specific and data-referenced
[ ] Input-to-output traceability confirmed

VERDICT
[ ] PASS — all five Vs clear. Deployment proceeds.
[ ] CONDITIONAL PASS — specific remediation required (listed below)
[ ] FAIL — one or more Vs failed. Deployment halted.

FAILURES (if any):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

---

### Stage 03 · Runtime Assessment
*Fired at: Every engagement or monitoring cycle*
*Owner: AI staff (automated), escalates to hirer and Joyn as defined*

At runtime, the 5V assessment is automated and continuous. The staff self-assesses against each V on every cycle and produces a runtime health signal. This signal is logged internally for monitoring and audit purposes. It is not typically surfaced to the hirer unless a Red condition persists, at which point it is escalated.

**Runtime health signals per V:**

| V | Green | Amber | Red |
|---|-------|-------|-----|
| **Veracity** | All sources verified, no conflicts detected | One source unverified or conflict detected and flagged | Source provenance broken or conflict silently resolved |
| **Value** | All outputs mapped to hirer needs, ROI signal positive | One output without clear hirer need mapping | Multiple outputs untraceable to hirer needs |
| **Vulnerability** | All data isolated, encrypted, within scope | Access scope approaching boundary | Data accessed outside defined scope or encryption gap detected |
| **Variability** | Output quality consistent with baseline | Quality drift detected, within tolerance | Quality drift exceeding tolerance or source instability confirmed |
| **Visibility** | Audit log complete, confidence tiers visible | Audit log gap detected | Audit log incomplete or confidence tiers missing from outputs |

**Escalation rules:**
- Any single V at Red → immediate escalation to hirer with specific description
- Two or more Vs at Amber simultaneously → escalation to hirer with summary
- Any V at Red for two consecutive cycles → escalation to Joyn (hire@tryjoyn.me) in addition to hirer
- Staff does not pause automatically on Amber — it flags and continues
- Staff pauses automatically on Red and awaits hirer instruction before resuming

---

### Stage 04 · Calibration Assessment
*Fired at: After each hirer feedback loop, before corpus update is committed*
*Owner: Calibration system, reviewed by staff and hirer*

At calibration stage, the 5V assessment acts as a quality gate on the corpus itself. Before any hirer feedback is committed to the calibration corpus, it is assessed against the five Vs. Low-quality signals — unverified, low-value, inconsistent, or opaque — are flagged before they corrupt the corpus.

**Calibration assessment questions per V:**

| V | Calibration question |
|---|---------------------|
| **Veracity** | Is this feedback based on verified information, or on the hirer's assumption? Is the feedback internally consistent with prior signals? |
| **Value** | Does this feedback improve future output quality? Or does it introduce noise? |
| **Vulnerability** | Does this feedback contain sensitive information about third parties that should not be stored in the corpus? |
| **Variability** | Is this feedback consistent with the hirer's prior calibration signals? If it conflicts, is the conflict explained? |
| **Visibility** | Is the feedback specific enough to be actionable? Can future outputs be traced to this calibration signal? |

**Pass condition:** Feedback committed to corpus with V assessment logged alongside it.

**Fail condition:** Feedback flagged and held. Hirer notified with specific question to resolve the flag before the signal is committed.

**The corpus quality principle:** A calibration corpus that has never had a signal rejected is a corpus that has never been governed. The 5V calibration assessment exists precisely to prevent low-quality signals from compounding into a permanently miscalibrated staff member.

## 03 — 5V and the DIKW Architecture

The five Vs map directly onto the DIKW pyramid defined in HUMAN-COLLAB.md §10:

| DIKW Layer | Primary Vs in play |
|------------|-------------------|
| **Data** | Veracity, Vulnerability — is the raw input trustworthy and secure? |
| **Information** | Value, Visibility — does the output serve a hirer need and can they see how it was produced? |
| **Knowledge** | Variability, Veracity — is the corpus consistent and based on verified signals? |
| **Wisdom** | All five — the human applies judgment informed by outputs that have passed all five Vs |

A hirer operating at the Wisdom layer can only exercise good judgment if the layers below them have been governed. The 5V framework is what makes the DIKW architecture trustworthy in practice, not just in theory.

## 04 — What Claude Reads This For

When Claude (any instance) is designing, building, or reviewing any Joyn staff or data-handling feature:

- Before specifying any data source → check V1 Veracity assessment questions
- Before writing any ROI chip or output claim → check V2 Value assessment questions
- Before designing any data storage or retention behaviour → check V3 Vulnerability
- Before designing any calibration mechanism → check V4 Variability calibration assessment
- Before designing any output display or audit trail → check V5 Visibility
- Before deploying any staff → run the Stage 02 deployment checklist in full
- Before committing any calibration signal → run the Stage 04 calibration assessment
- If a runtime health signal turns Red → follow Stage 03 escalation rules exactly
