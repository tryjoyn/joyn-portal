Probe is designed for insurance teams that have ideas worth testing but no capacity to run them properly. Every experiment is structured — hypothesis defined, controls set, success criteria agreed — before a single test is run.

The team

Five agents.
One structured debrief.

01 · Intake & Framing
Frame
Hypothesis Director

Processes your innovation brief, sharpens the hypothesis into a testable statement, and defines scope, constraints, and success criteria before any experiment design begins. Every engagement starts and ends with Frame.

Brief processing Hypothesis sharpening Scope definition
02 · Market Signals
Scout
Signal Intelligence

Surveys the landscape before you build anything — existing products, competitor moves, adjacent market precedents, and regulatory constraints relevant to the hypothesis. Flags where others have tried, what worked, and what the regulatory exposure is.

Competitor mapping Regulatory exposure Market precedent
03 · Experiment Design
Spec
Test Architecture

Designs the experiment — methodology, control group, sample size, timeline, and measurement framework. Produces the experiment spec your team approves before anything runs. Flags design flaws that would invalidate findings before they cost time and money.

Test methodology Control design Measurement framework
04 · Execution Tracking
Watch
Live Monitoring

Monitors the experiment as it runs — tracking against the approved spec, flagging deviations, data quality issues, and early signals. Raises a flag the moment something material changes mid-experiment so you can make an informed decision on whether to continue, pivot, or stop.

Deviation detection Data quality Early signal alerts
05 · Synthesis & Debrief
Debrief
Learning & Recommendation

Receives structured findings from all four domain agents. Synthesises into a single coherent debrief — what was tested, what the data shows, what it means for your business, and what to do next. Produces the output your leadership reviews and the institutional knowledge your firm keeps. Every finding is traceable back to the agent that produced it.

Engagement model

Three modes for
three stages of innovation.

01
Feasibility Scan
Pre-experiment · 48 hours

Frame and Scout assess the hypothesis before you commit to running it. Landscape survey, regulatory exposure, comparable precedents, and a clear view of whether the idea is worth testing. Know before you spend.

02
Live Experiment
Active test · 2–4 weeks

Full five-agent engagement. Hypothesis sharpened, experiment designed, execution monitored, and debrief produced. Your practitioners direct at every intervention point — the team handles the structured work in between. Findings are yours to keep.

03
Innovation Portfolio
Annual programme · Ongoing

A managed pipeline of experiments running across the year — scoped, sequenced, and debriefed on a cadence that fits your planning cycle. Probe tracks what was learned and what to test next. Institutional learning compounds over time.
