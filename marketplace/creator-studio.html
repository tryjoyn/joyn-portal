How we encode expertise

Five layers. One cohesive staff member.

1
Domain
The substantive knowledge of the field — what matters, what doesn't, and why
2
Methodology
Your specific approach — the sequence, the frameworks, the way you think through problems
3
Workflow
The operational steps — what triggers each action, what gets handed off and when
4
Voice
How the staff member communicates — the tone, the register, the language of the field
5
Standards
The quality bars — what counts as a good output, and what falls short

Every Joyn AI staff member is built through a structured knowledge transfer — not a prompt, not a fine-tune. You work with the Joyn team to articulate each of the five layers in your domain. The Joyn team handles the technical implementation.

This process takes 4–8 weeks for most roles. The depth of transfer is what separates a Joyn staff member from a generic AI assistant. The more precisely you can articulate your methodology, the higher the quality of the final product.

Once built, your staff member is tested against real scenarios before going to market. You review the outputs. You sign off on the quality. Your name is on it.
