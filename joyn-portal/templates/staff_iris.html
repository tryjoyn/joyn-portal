2026-03-04
Florida OIR Bulletin 2026-04 — Continuing Education Requirements
CE credit hours increased from 18 to 24. New September deadline. 50% online cap introduced.
Medium View
Summary

Florida OIR has issued Bulletin 2026-04 revising continuing education requirements for all licensed agents holding a Property & Casualty or Life & Health licence. Required annual CE credit hours increase from 18 to 24, effective for the 2026–2027 licence renewal cycle. The compliance deadline shifts from 31 December to 30 September each year, giving the OIR time to process renewals before year-end. All existing credits earned prior to this bulletin continue to count toward the new total.

What Changed
Before — Bulletin 2025-11
CE hours18 credit hours / year
Deadline31 December each year
Ethics2 hours (of 18)
Online capNo limit
After — Bulletin 2026-04
CE hours24 credit hours / year
Deadline30 September each year
Ethics4 hours (of 24)
Online capMax 12 hours (50%)
What This Means for Your Agents

Your Florida-licensed agents need 6 additional CE hours per year beginning with the 2026–2027 renewal cycle. The deadline shift is the more urgent change — agents who left CE completion to Q4 now have until 30 September. Any agent with a renewal window between October and December must complete all 24 hours by the prior September. Recommend notifying your Florida-licensed team before Q2 2026. The new 50% online cap (12 of 24 hours) may require some agents to source in-person or classroom credits if they currently rely heavily on online courses.

Why Iris Flagged This

This bulletin directly affects your monitored line (P&C, Florida) and carries a compliance action requirement for all licensed agents. The deadline change from December to September shrinks the effective compliance window by 3 months, creating a real risk of inadvertent lapses. Failure to comply results in licence suspension — which is why this was raised at medium severity rather than low, despite no immediate penalty date.

Citizens Property Insurance — 11.2% Rate Increase Filing
Statewide residential homeowners rate increase filed. OIR has 90 days to decide. Private market repricing likely to follow.
High View
Summary

Citizens Property Insurance Corporation has filed for an average statewide rate increase of 11.2% on residential homeowners policies, effective 1 July 2026. The filing covers HO-3 and HO-6 forms across all Florida counties. The OIR has 90 days to approve, modify, or reject the filing. Citizens typically serves as the insurer of last resort — but rate increases of this scale create market-wide repricing signals and often trigger private market rate reviews within 60–90 days of approval.

What This Means for Your Agents

If approved, Florida homeowners clients will see an average 11.2% premium increase — with coastal counties potentially higher. This is an opportunity as much as a risk: clients receiving renewal notices with increased premiums will actively be shopping. Prepare a private market comparison brief for your top Florida homeowners accounts in Q2 2026. Private carriers typically file their own increases within 60 days of a Citizens approval — Iris will brief you if and when that happens.

Why Iris Flagged This

Citizens rate filings carry market-wide implications well beyond Citizens policyholders. Flagged at high severity because: (1) the 11.2% average masks significant county-level variation — some areas may see 15–20%; (2) approval is likely given Citizens' loss ratio trajectory; (3) the OIR 90-day review window means this becomes actionable for client conversations in the next 4–6 weeks. The window for proactive client outreach closes once approval is announced.
